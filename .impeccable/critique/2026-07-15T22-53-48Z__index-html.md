## Anti-Patterns Verdict

But three of the explicitly-banned tells stack on the same elements: numbered `01–06` card indices on the 6 feature cards and `01–04` on the 4 security cards; identical uppercase-tracked mono kickers above all 8 sections (`MYBILLS WRAPPED`, `A IDEIA É SIMPLES`, `RELATÓRIOS`, `SEGURANÇA`...); and mono used off-brief as label text (DESIGN.md reserves mono strictly for money/numbers). The LLM review and detector agree strongly on three things: the numbered-index/kicker repetition, the layout-property transitions, and (once browser evidence is in) the contrast gap. ## Overall Impression

The page has real craft under the hood — the copy voice, the plans scroll-swap + static pricing grid combo, and the FAQ placement all show deliberate interaction-design thinking, not template-filling. But it's fighting itself in two places: a CTA system with four different visual treatments for one action, and a motion budget (ten simultaneous systems: particles, parallax, two pinned scroll-jacks, magnetic buttons, tilt, shimmer, blur-text, scroll-float, grain) that undercuts the "sério" positioning it's trying to sell. The single biggest opportunity is tightening the CTA vocabulary to one primary treatment — it would fix a Nielsen heuristic gap, remove the highest-visibility contributor to the "template" read, and directly serve the belief-ladder's highest-stakes moment.

## What's Working

- **The plans scroll-swap → static pricing grid sequence** turns plan comparison into a narrative beat first, then resolves the actual side-by-side decision immediately after — solves engagement and usability without sacrificing either.
- **FAQ placement and content** directly preempt the classic subscription fears (pegadinha, cartão-pra-testar, cancelamento, dados bancários) at exactly the point in the funnel where anxiety peaks, right before the final CTA.
- **Copy voice** ("Zero asteriscos.", "Prometido.", "Feito pra mim. Bom o bastante pra qualquer um.") is consistently informal and non-corporate without tipping into juvenile — harder to land than it looks, and it's landed.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Inconsistent CTA styling dilutes the primary action**
- **Why it matters**: four different button treatments — nav's flat "Começar agora" (line 748), star-border gradient "Ver planos →" / "Criar minha conta →" (lines 819, 1654), and three differently-colored pricing-card buttons (flat surface / solid teal / solid purple-dark, lines 1417–1481) — all mean "assinar." At the highest-stakes decision point this adds recognition friction instead of removing it, and it's the direct cause of the Consistency heuristic scoring a 2/4.
- **Fix**: reserve the star-border gradient exclusively for the one true primary CTA. Give the three pricing-card buttons one consistent chrome, differentiated only by the accent color each plan badge already uses.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify` (CTA hierarchy is a copy+affordance problem first) or `/impeccable layout`

**[P1] Contrast failures across 11 confirmed instances, not just one token**
- **Why it matters**: `--ink-dimmer` (#64748B) on the dark backgrounds sits around 3.5–3.8:1, and even `--accent` (#8B5CF6) on `--bg` is 4.1:1 — both below the 4.5:1 AA floor for normal-weight text. This hits captions, plan labels, and footer copy directly, and three flagged elements report text-on-identical-background (1.0:1) that needs a manual look to rule out a genuine invisible-text bug.
- **Fix**: bump `--ink-dimmer` a few steps lighter or restrict it to strictly decorative/non-essential text; verify the three 1.0:1 flags aren't a real bug (check `#planPanel0/1/2` and `.star-inner` computed styles).
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable audit`

**[P2] Numbered card indices + uppercase mono kickers, repeated 8+ times, are the strongest template tell on the page**
- **Why it matters**: `01`–`06` on feature cards, `01`–`04` on security cards, and an identical uppercase-tracked mono kicker above all 8 section headers are exactly the scaffolding patterns the brand register explicitly bans as AI-default grammar — confirmed by both the LLM review and the detector's `numbered-section-markers` and `repeated-section-kickers` (×7) findings. Mono is also being used off-brief here: DESIGN.md's Number-Is-Mono Rule reserves it for money/numbers, not labels.
- **Fix**: drop the sequence numerals from feature/security cards; vary the section-intro treatment instead of repeating kicker+h2+p identically 8 times.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable typeset` or `/impeccable quieter`

**[P2] Belief-ladder stage 1 ("meu problema é real") is never articulated**
- **Why it matters**: the hero and every following section speak from the solution/differentiation frame; no copy names the reader's actual pain (spreadsheet fatigue, feature-gated apps) in their own words before pivoting to the pitch. That's a gap at the very first rung of the belief ladder PRODUCT.md defines.
- **Fix**: add one line in the hero subhead (or immediately after) that names the felt problem before the pivot to "e por isso fizemos o MyBills."
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify`

**[P2] No `prefers-reduced-motion` handling anywhere**
- **Why it matters**: the page runs particles, parallax blobs, two pinned scroll-jacked sections, magnetic buttons, tilt, shimmer, blur-text and scroll-float simultaneously — zero matches for `prefers-reduced-motion` in the stylesheet. That's a real accessibility gap for vestibular-sensitive users on a page this motion-dense, not a nice-to-have.
- **Fix**: wrap the parallax/tilt/magnetic/pinned-scroll JS in a `matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)')` check and ship static equivalents.
- **Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden` or `/impeccable animate`

## Minor Observations

- The `#foco` pinned 230vh scroll-scale section is a heavy investment for one line of copy — consider shortening it or making it a lighter, non-pinned reveal.
- The responsive CSS block's own comment admits the architecture is fragile ("every override needs `!important` to win against inline layout declarations") — a maintainability smell (100% inline styles + `!important` media queries) worth addressing even though it isn't user-visible today.
- `#tour` screenshots have no `loading="lazy"` or explicit width/height reservation beyond `aspect-ratio` — possible blank-frame flash on slow connections.
- `layout-transition` findings (×7, both CLI and browser-confirmed): `transition: width/height/max-height` on `#planBar` and the FAQ accordion animate layout properties directly; converting to `transform`/`grid-template-rows` would be smoother and matches the General Rules motion guidance already in the skill.
- `em-dash-overuse` (5 in body copy) — worth a proofreading pass if it wasn't a deliberate voice choice.
- The cyan hits in the detector's `ai-color-palette` finding don't map to any documented DESIGN.md token — worth tracing where that color is coming from (possibly `particles.js` or an untracked gradient) before assuming it's a false positive.
